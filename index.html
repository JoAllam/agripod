🌾 About the Project
Our Mission

Growing more
with less.

Egypt feeds over 105 million people — yet faces mounting pressure from water scarcity and harsh climate conditions. Conventional farming methods are struggling to keep pace.

AgriPod proposes an automated, closed-loop feedback control system for smart greenhouse production. By continuously monitoring temperature, soil moisture, and light intensity, the system deploys targeted actuators to keep all three parameters within optimal ranges — entirely without human intervention.

Built from recycled materials and designed to run 6+ hours autonomously, AgriPod is a scalable, cost-effective answer to food insecurity — replicable across Egypt and developing nations worldwide.

How We Built It
Phase 1 — Pre-Construction
Research & 3D Design
Defined the three core parameters. Created a 3D model of the prototype to finalize structural decisions — greenhouse material, soil type, sensor selection, and spatial layout.
Phase 2 — Prototype Build
Constructing the Greenhouse
Recycled a glass cubic container as the greenhouse body. Installed the DC fan, layered cork + mesh + peat moss, glued the LED strip inside, and assembled the solenoid valve with a simulated drip-irrigation system fed by a mounted 600ml bottle.
Phase 3 — Electronics
Wiring the Circuit
Connected an ESP32 to a breadboard with the DHT22, LDR, and soil moisture sensors. Wired three relays between the 12V power supply and the actuators, allowing the ESP32 to safely control high-voltage components without damage.
Phase 4 — Calibration
Sensor Accuracy & Tuning
Cross-referenced DHT22 readings with a weather app. Calibrated the LDR with a luxmeter in the physics lab. Calibrated the soil sensor in the geology lab using air (dry) and water (wet) reference points, then mapped readings to a 0–100% scale.
Phase 5 — Testing
Real-World Validation
Ran 6-hour autonomous tests across three plant species. Recorded readings at 9:00, 14:00, and 19:00 over five consecutive days outdoors. Applied three deliberate deviations per parameter and measured recovery time. System successfully maintained all three parameters simultaneously and in real time.
